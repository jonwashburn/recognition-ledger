\subsection*{Technical Foundation}

Let $\rho_R(\mathbf{x},t)$ denote the density of recognitions per effective voxel, where each voxel has characteristic length $\lambda_{\text{eff}} \approx 60\,\mu\text{m}$ set by sparse ledger occupancy. Let $\psi(\mathbf{x},t)$ encode the multi--body entanglement depth measured in bits of mutual information. The local urgency potential becomes:

\begin{equation}
\urgency(\mathbf{x},t) = \tau_0\,\frac{\partial}{\partial t} \ln\bigl[\psi(\mathbf{x},t)\,\rho_R(\mathbf{x},t)\bigr]
\end{equation}

\noindent where $\tau_0 = 5.391 \times 10^{-44}\,\text{s}$ is the cosmic tick length. This urgency modulates the local acceleration scale according to:

\begin{equation}
a_0(\mathbf{x},t) = a_{0,\text{cosmic}}\left[1 + \kappa\,\frac{\urgency(\mathbf{x},t)}{\urgency_0}\right]
\end{equation}

\noindent with $a_{0,\text{cosmic}} = 1.2 \times 10^{-10}\,\text{m}\,\text{s}^{-2}$ (the MOND scale), $\kappa \approx 1$ the coupling constant to be measured, and $\urgency_0$ the cosmic mean urgency.

The modified Poisson equation for the gravitational potential $\Phi$ becomes:
\begin{equation}
\nabla^2\Phi = 4\pi G\rho + \nabla \cdot \left[\frac{a_0(\mathbf{x},t)}{|\nabla\Phi|}\,\nabla\Phi\right]
\end{equation}

\noindent reducing to Newtonian gravity when $|\nabla\Phi| \gg a_0$ and to the deep--MOND regime when $|\nabla\Phi| \ll a_0$.
